Preparation

  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %}Snow foam is applied to loosen dirt and lubricate surfaces.
  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %}Vehicle is hand washed using a two buck system, this ensures no additional markings are put into the paint.

Decontaimination

  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} The cleaned surface is then rid of any tar, iron fallout, and surface level bugs. All of which are extremley common contaiminates from the road and environment.
  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} The vehicle is then wiped over with a clay bar, similar to molding clay or dense putty. This process uplifts any remaining contaiminates that are embeded within the paints top coat.

Restoration

  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} The cleaned and fully decontaiminated surface can now be inspected to determine what unique level of restoration is required to reveal high quality paint.
  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} The surface will receive one or multiple stages of abrasive compound which removes imperfections, reveling the paints original quality.
  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} Finally, the surface is then polished with a fine grit compound that will refine the paint, further enhancing gloss and luster.

Protection

  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} The vehicles newly restored paint is then given a layer protection based on the owners desired outcome. Ranging from 3-6 months to upwards of a year in protection and hydrophobic qualities.
  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} Our highest level of protection, ceramic coating offers maximum protection and hydrophobicity (fancy way of saying extremly water resistant).
  • {% include social-icon.html icon="ProcessSVG" %} Ceramic coatings provide 12 months + of protection and makes future washes effortless due to the hydrophobic qualities that don't allow any future dirt, debris, and water adhere to paint.
